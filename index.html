Introduction

Physics helps us understand how matter and energy work in our world. From solids, liquids, and gases to heat, electricity, and magnetism, physics explains how things move, change, and interact. On this website, we will learn about density and pressure, heat transfer and thermal energy, electric current and circuits, and magnetism and electromagnetism. Step by step, we will explore how energy flows and how forces work in everyday life.

What We Explore

Grade 7 – Solid, Liquid, Gases

Explore the states of matter, solids, liquids, and gases, and learn key concepts such as density, pressure, hydrostatic pressure, and Pascal's principle to understand how fluids and forces work in everyday life.

Grade 8 – Thermal Physics

Learn how heat and temperature affect matter through topics such as heat capacity, latent heat, heating and cooling curves, Black's principle, and heat transfer (conduction, convection, and radiation).

Grade 9 Semester 1 – Dynamic Electricity

Discover how electricity flows in circuits by studying electric potential, electric current, Ohm's law, Kirchhoff's law, electromotive force, resistors, and series and parallel circuits.

Grade 9 Semester 2 – Magnetism

Learn about magnets and magnetic materials, magnetic poles, magnetic induction, magnetization and demagnetization, magnetic fields, magnetic fields produced by electric currents, and electromagnetism along with some common uses of electromagnets.
